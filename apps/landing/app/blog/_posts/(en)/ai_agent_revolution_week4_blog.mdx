![Real-Time AI Video Processing](/images/blog-posts/mirage-lsd-real-time-video.jpeg)

Decart has launched MirageLSD, an AI video model that transforms live video feeds in real-time, solving two critical problems that have plagued AI video tools: slow rendering speeds and rapid quality degradation over time. Unlike traditional AI video generators that create entire sequences at once and struggle with temporal consistency, MirageLSD takes a revolutionary frame-by-frame approach that maintains quality throughout extended sessions. The breakthrough comes after six months of intensive development following Decart's viral Minecraft project Oasis, demonstrating the startup's commitment to pushing the boundaries of real-time AI applications. What makes this particularly impressive is the sub-40 millisecond processing time per frame, achieved through innovative techniques like architecture-aware pruning specifically optimized for Nvidia Hopper GPUs. This represents a 16x performance improvement over previous methods, bringing AI video transformation from the realm of offline processing into truly interactive applications. The technology addresses a fundamental challenge in AI video generation: maintaining coherence and quality as videos extend beyond the typical 5-10 second clips that most models can handle before visual degradation becomes apparent.

### **Revolutionary Features:**
- **Ultra-Low Latency**: Each frame processed in under 40 milliseconds
- **Real-Time Performance**: 20 frames per second at 768 x 432 resolution
- **Frame-by-Frame Generation**: Creates each frame individually rather than entire sequences
- **Quality Stability**: Uses "diffusion forcing" and "history augmentation" to prevent error accumulation
- **Optimized Performance**: 16x performance boost through architecture-aware pruning and shortcut distillation

### **Technical Innovation:**
- **Diffusion Forcing**: Adds noise to individual frames, teaching the model to clean up images without relying on previous frames
- **History Augmentation**: Trains the model on distorted frames to recognize and correct recurring mistakes
- **Nvidia Hopper Optimization**: Specifically tuned for maximum efficiency on cutting-edge hardware

### **Platform Availability:**
The Mirage platform is live with web access, and iOS/Android apps are coming soon. Target applications include livestreaming, video calls, and gaming, with regular summer updates planned.

![AI Intelligence Benchmark Testing](/images/blog-posts/arc-agi-3-benchmark.jpeg)

The ARC Prize foundation has unveiled ARC-AGI-3, an Interactive Reasoning Benchmark (IRB) designed to measure human-like intelligence in AI systems through skill-acquisition efficiency in novel environments. This isn't just another benchmark â€“ it's a complete paradigm shift away from static tests that have dominated AI evaluation for years, toward dynamic assessments that capture the full spectrum of intelligent behavior. Created by FranÃ§ois Chollet, the benchmark recognizes that true intelligence is inherently interactive and unfolds over time through exploration, planning, reflection, and adaptation. The foundation's bold stance is clear: "As long as that gap remains between human and artificial intelligence, we do not have AGI." Unlike previous benchmarks that could be gamed through memorization or pattern matching, ARC-AGI-3 uses hand-crafted novel game environments that require genuine understanding and learning efficiency. The benchmark will feature approximately 100 unique environments split between public and private evaluation sets, where AI agents must demonstrate sustained reasoning over multiple steps without prior instructions. This represents the most ambitious attempt yet to create an objective measure for when we've truly achieved artificial general intelligence, moving beyond impressive demos to measurable, repeatable assessment of human-level cognitive capabilities.

### **Paradigm Shift:**
- **Beyond Static Tests**: Moves away from traditional benchmarks to measure the full spectrum of intelligence
- **Interactive Focus**: Tests exploration, perception-to-action pipelines, memory, goal acquisition, and alignment
- **Human Baseline**: Declares AGI achievable when artificial systems match human learning efficiency
- **Game-Based Testing**: Uses ~100 unique game environments split between public and private evaluation sets

### **Key Principles:**
- **Core Knowledge Priors**: No reliance on language, trivia, or vast training data
- **Generalization Focus**: Tests ability to adapt to completely novel situations
- **Efficiency Measurement**: Evaluates how quickly systems can acquire new skills
- **Multi-Step Reasoning**: Requires perception, decision-making, and action over extended sequences

### **Development Timeline:**
- **Current Status**: Early preview with 6 games (3 public, 3 releasing August 2025)
- **Full Launch**: Set for 2026 with complete benchmark dataset
- **Community Involvement**: Open call for game ideas and agent development through HuggingFace partnership

### **The Stakes:**
As the foundation states: "As long as that gap remains between human and artificial intelligence, we do not have AGI." This benchmark could become the definitive measure of when we've truly achieved artificial general intelligence.
