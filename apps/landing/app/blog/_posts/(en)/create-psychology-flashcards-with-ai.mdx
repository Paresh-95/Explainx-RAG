## Creating Flashcards That Stick

**Creating flashcards** effectively requires strategy. Follow these psychology-proven principles:

**One concept per card.** Don't overload cards with multiple definitions. Create separate cards for related concepts like short-term memory, working memory, and long-term memory.

**Use clear questions.** Instead of writing "Operant Conditioning," ask "What's the difference between positive and negative reinforcement?" This trains specific recall abilities.

**Add real examples.** Connect abstract theories to concrete situations. A card about social learning theory becomes memorable when you include examples like children imitating aggressive behavior from video games.
